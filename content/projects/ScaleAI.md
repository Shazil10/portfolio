An empirical study of the inter-arrival times between ball-out-of-play events in Boca Juniors Feminine matches, treating them as realizations of a random process. Uses descriptive statistics, histogram/PDF fits, CDF comparisons, Q–Q plots, and boxplots to compare Exponential vs Gamma models, concluding that evolving match conditions produce waiting times best captured by a Gamma distribution rather than a simple memoryless process.
